The purpose of IT in modern business

In a 24 hour society time has become critical.
We simply do not have enough hours in the day to achieve everything that we want to.
As a result IT has become crucial to all aspects of our daily lives.
We order our shopping online, use ticket machines for our travel, even keep in touch with friends and family online, all in an effort to save time.

The same applies for business, as time is money.
The more efficient a business, the higher the turnover, this is where IT becomes essential.
With technology marketing has become both easier and more successful,
able to reach a wider range of customers with minimal effort.
Businesses are now better able to make decisions based on solid market research which previously wasn’t available.
Planning has become easier with tools such as kanban boards to solve complex problems,
which are easily accessible from work or at home, making organizing and delegating tasks easier.
Many businesses are now able to offer 24/7 support through web chat services that were previously unavailable.
This allows businesses to maintain good customer relations with their customers.
Resource management has improved through IT, now users are able to access software from Cloud storage.

What's needed to make a project successful?

To successfully design a project, planning is essential which is made easier with kanban tools such as trello.
Trello allows you to set goals, assign individual tasks and have a general overview of where you are up to and what needs to be done with the project.
This allows for clear project goals, and for the design team to know their purpose and what they’re working towards.
Communication is also key, a good way to keep in contact with your colleagues is slack.
Slack can easily be integrated with trello to provide you with updates whenever a task has been set or completed.
This allows a project manager to assign resources to each individual task, allowing them to more effectively lead a team.

What causes digital projects to fail?

Over Ambition can cause projects to fail, often companies seek perfection in the project that can cause significant delays to the release date,
causing the cost of development to exceed the given budget, in more severe cases it can even drive a company into bankruptcy.
This is caused by lack of planning and not acknowledging the limits of what you can do, it’s expectation vs reality.
A project also needs to be flexible to adapt to changes in the business or market, what we need today may not essentially be what we need tomorrow,
projects fail or are cancelled due to this reason, which is why planning in the early stages is essential.

It is important to firstly know what you’re trying to achieve,
what your end goal is, from there you can research every tool that you need to complete the job.
Will I be creating something that solely works on iOS?

This applies for every project as it’s essential to start correctly, mistakes cost the business money, and can inevitably lead to projects failing.

Legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

General Data Protection Regulation came into effect May 25th 2018,
which means any personal data such as names, email addresses, photos, residential address, bank details, medical info, IP addresses are all protected.
Giving customers the right to:
Access - Meaning they can request information on how there data is being used.
To be forgotton - If the customer no longer wishes to be affiliated with the company
Data portability - To transfer there information to a different company
Customers are to be informed before data is collected on them.
The right to have data corrected if it’s incorrect.
Restrict processing so companies can store their information but not use it.
The right to object to their data being used for marketing purposes
The right to be notified of any data breaches.

This means that companies have to be more vigilant than ever during market research which is essential to any digital project, or they could risk facing heavy fines that could negatively impact their business More information found here

Copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright means the right to re-produce work, to prepare derivative works, to distribute copies,
to perform the work, and display the work publically.
If you are using content that has been copyrighted and you don't legally have the right to use the content you can be fined
A good way to avoid any copy right issues is when using google to find images you can change the settings to filter out copyrighted images.
When quoting from a website it is always a good idea to include a link to their website as well.
For more details please see: bitlaw.com

How people with disabilities use the web

People who are blind often use screen readers that reads the text on the screen, or refreshable braile displays,
we can help them by using text based browsers rather than graphical browsers.
However people with low vision can be helped by increasing our text font size and making sure we use colors that stand out,
so our text is not similiar to our background.
The same applies to people with colour blindness, making sure we are not using similar colours as it'll make it difficult for them tl read.
Seizure disorders can be a common problem when using websites that have flickering gifs or sudden flashes.
I used still images for this reason rather than moving gifs to make it more accessible.
Also people with psychriatic disabilities have trouble focusing so I made sure there was no distracting visual or audio elements to my site
